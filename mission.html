The Upward Literacy & Intellect Project (TULIP)

Inception

    TULIP is a nonprofit organization based in New York City, specifically Northern Queens, that focuses on residents’ educational development in literacy and language. TULIP is an acronym for The Upward Literacy and Intellect Project. The organization recognizes and works to amend the weaknesses of the New York City Public School System’s coherence in teaching the English Language Arts: the public school system does not place a great enough emphasis on grammar. For example, in 1985, the National Council of Teachers of English (NCTE) began to discourage instruction on grammar. Despite there being reason in the NCTE’s arguments that grammar is a fluid subject so declaring it “proper” or not is difficult, grammar still appears in everyday writing. Commas are used daily. There is a period at the end of every sentence. And every sentence has a subject and a verb, perhaps even an object.

Mission

To reinvigorate the joy of academia and learning through increased literacy.

Vision

To nurture a sense of community through learned interest in further exploration of the English language.
