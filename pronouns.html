Pronouns

Types of Pronouns

Subject Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, and they

Object Pronouns: me, you, him, her, it, us, and them.

Possessive Pronouns: my/mine, your/yours, his, her/hers, its, our/ours, and their/theirs

Reflexive Pronouns: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, and themselves

Subject vs Object

Is this correct? “Me and him go to the store.”
What are the subjects of the sentence? "Me and him"
What are the pronouns used to address the subjects? Object
The rules: If you use “I” in the sentence, you have to be the last subject. You have to use subject pronouns for the subjects of a sentence.
Rewrite the sentence: He and I go to the store. Use object pronouns for the objects of a sentence (something that the subject does something to)
Example: He fights her.
