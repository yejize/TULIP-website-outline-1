Conjunctions

What are conjuctions?

Conjuctions are words/phrases that connect two clauses and/or sentences.

There are three types of conjunctions:

- Coordinating Conjunctions: (FANBOYS) For; And; Nor; But; Or; Yet; So

- Subordinating Conjunctions: After; Although; Until; While; Before; Because; and more

- Correlative Conjunctions : Either/or; So/that; Both/and; Rather/than; Whether/or; and more

Coordinating Conjunctions

F - For A- And N - Nor B- But O- Or Y- Yet S- So When there is an independent clause and another independent clause connected by a fanboy, be sure to use a comma before the fanboy

Example: I am not feeling well, but I will still work.

Correlative Conjunctions

Either/or So/that Both/and Rather/than Whether/or + more…Either/or So/that Both/and Rather/than Whether/or + more…

These conjunctions are paired together but adhere to the other rules of conjunctions and commas.

Example: Either you do the dishes, or you leave. I learned so much that my head hurts.
